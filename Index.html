The Reality

The remodeling industry is broken for honest contractors.

If you've been doing this for more than a year, you've experienced at least one of these. Probably all of them.

💸
Chasing unpaid invoices
You did the work. You showed up every day. You delivered what you promised. And then the homeowner finds reasons to withhold the final payment. You spend weeks chasing money that's legally yours, with no system to back you up.
Avg. contractor spends 3.2hrs/week chasing payments
📉
Losing bids to cut-rate competitors
You price the job correctly — materials, labor, permits, insurance overhead. Someone else bids 30% less, skips the permit, uses inferior materials, and gets the job. The homeowner finds out six months later. You never get the chance to prove the difference.
73% of quality contractors cite underbidding as their #1 challenge
🤷
Floating materials costs
Cabinets need to be ordered 8 weeks out. Custom countertop slabs require a deposit. Lumber for framing needs to be on site before work starts. That's $15,000–$40,000 out of your pocket before you've received a single progress payment.
Avg. material float before first payment: $22,000
📋
Scope creep with no paper trail
The homeowner asks for "just a few small additions" throughout the project. You do them because you want the relationship to go well. At the end, they remember none of them and refuse to pay for the extra work. You have no documentation. They have no memory.
Scope creep accounts for 41% of contractor payment disputes
⭐
One bad review defines you
You have 50 satisfied clients who never wrote a review. One unreasonable client leaves a one-star review on Google and it tanks your rating. No context. No verification. No recourse. Competitors with worse work but more aggressive review solicitation outrank you.
67% of contractors say unfair reviews have cost them projects
🔌
Lead platforms that don't care if you win
Angi charges you for leads whether they convert or not. HomeAdvisor sells the same lead to five contractors. Houzz charges $299/month regardless of your project volume. None of them have any stake in whether you actually get hired — or paid.
Avg. contractor wastes $4,200/yr on unconverted paid leads

Your Protections

The system is neutral. Not homeowner-sided.

Every protection built for homeowners has a corresponding protection for contractors. Here's what that looks like.

⏱️
72-hour auto-approval
If a homeowner doesn't respond to your milestone submission within 72 hours, funds release automatically. Homeowners cannot use silence as leverage to delay your payment indefinitely.
🕐
24-hour first response window
When a homeowner initiates a review, you get 24 hours to respond before any payment hold is applied. If your response resolves the concern, the hold never triggers. You're treated as a professional, not a suspect.
📋
Industry standard as the quality benchmark
Quality disputes are evaluated against TCNA, PDCA, NWFA, and other trade standards — not homeowner preference. Work that meets the applicable standard cannot be disputed on aesthetic grounds alone. This is in writing in the homeowner agreement they sign before contracting.
⚖️
Bad-faith homeowner consequences
Three unfounded review requests triggers a formal platform review of the homeowner's account. Homeowners who initiate reviews and go silent lose their ability to pause payments. Their dispute rights are tied to their conduct — just like yours are.
📝
Your rebuttal evidence is weighted equally
When a review is filed, you submit your own evidence package — additional photos, industry standard references, inspection certificates, supplier confirmations. Your rebuttal sits alongside the homeowner's concern. Neither starts with presumed credibility.
🏆
Score-based benefit of the doubt
Master Contractors with a score above 90 get an expedited review track, a higher threshold for escalating to third-party inspection, and a cost structure on inspections that reflects their earned trust. Your track record works for you.
💬
Contractor-initiated review requests
If a homeowner is stalling on a milestone approval without cause, you can request a Resolution Specialist to reach out on your behalf. You're not stuck waiting — you have a path to action too.
✅
Proactive resolution rewards
If you offer a credit or repair within 24 hours of a review being opened — before any specialist involvement — the review is marked "resolved by contractor" rather than "dispute filed." Your profile stays clean. Your score stays high.
